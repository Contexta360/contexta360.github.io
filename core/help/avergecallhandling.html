Average handle time, sometimes abbreviated AHT is one of the most important metrics used in call centres to improve agent efficiency and enhance customer satisfaction. It is a key measure to enable contact centres measure how long customer interactions take, not just the talk time but the complete journey from introduction to any hold time and 'wrap-up'. It can identify how much time an agent spends working on a call and when they are unable to deal with a new task. It can also be used to identify staffing levels.
Contact centres use the AHT metric to determine if additional training or hiring is needed. For example, if average handle time has been slowly creeping up over time, it may be indicative of growth in call volume which will require more capable agents to handle the calls. AHT is also frequently used to determine if a contact centre is functioning efficiently.

The more time an agent is spending on a single call, the fewer calls they're able to handle. Knowing your AHT can help you identify root cause to whether you need to work to reduce the time spent on each call.
